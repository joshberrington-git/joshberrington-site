Unilever · SheaMoisture · 2019–2021

A Celebration, Not a Turnaround

Joining a brand with real craft and a loyal following, then helping that work find a bigger spotlight.

I joined SheaMoisture Men about two years into my time at Unilever, with real admiration for what the team had already built: a brand with genuine craft, a devoted following, and products made for men who had never been the default in the grooming aisle. I didn't arrive to fix it. I arrived to help it shine.

Helping it shine meant getting into all of it. I revamped the product formulations and rebuilt the sales strategy, from the trade program to how we won the endcap. I sold the line into national and regional retailers and reworked the P&L behind it. I redrew the five-year innovation roadmap. And I launched the campaign that would put the brand on the map.

That campaign was #DefineYourBeard. We invited fifteen men from different backgrounds to do something simple: define confidence and wellness on their own terms, and celebrate a little beard love while they were at it. Then the internet did the rest.

It went viral, and crucially, in exactly the rooms we wanted to be in. The work landed with the publications and creators whose audiences we were actually trying to reach, from mainstream tastemakers to the culture and grooming corners of the internet, all the way through to the retail trade. Revenue grew 5x in under eighteen months, we 4x'd our points of distribution, and the products earned industry awards for their formulations.

But the growth was the output, not the point. What stays with me is what happens when you treat a brand, and the community behind it, with genuine respect, and put real craft behind letting it shine. The business case was the proof, not the motivation.
