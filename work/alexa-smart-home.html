Amazon · Alexa Smart Home · 2024–Now

Leading GTM for AI-Powered Smart Home

The launch narrative for Alexa+ Smart Home, and the agent infrastructure built to support it.

I lead go-to-market strategy for Alexa Smart Home's AI-powered hardware and software features, the layer of the business where the devices people already trust in their homes start getting genuinely smarter. That includes owning the launch narrative for Alexa+ Smart Home, the story that has to land with press, partners, and customers all at once.

Bringing an AI-native product to market requires a different motion than a traditional hardware launch: the value isn't a spec sheet, it's a behavior change, and the narrative has to make an invisible, evolving capability feel concrete enough to want. That meant rebuilding the GTM playbook itself, from positioning to proof points to sequencing, for a product that keeps getting smarter after it ships.

The work didn't stop at the narrative. I transformed how I work for an agentic era, putting AI agents at the center of my own workflow, then led a division-wide initiative to do the same across Alexa: rethinking how teams operate, and how the org thinks about work itself, as agents become part of how things get done. What began as a personal shift became an organization-wide transformation.

The thread connecting both: in an AI-native world, marketing leaders can't just narrate the transformation. They have to be early, credible practitioners of it.

Selected wins

Positioning

The Smart Home Starter Kit

4.6★ customer rating Zero creative budget Gen AI + CGI creative 100% organic

None of it was new. The Echo Hub, the Smart Plug, the four Amazon Basics smart bulbs: every component already existed and already sold on its own. What didn't exist was the idea, a single, obvious on-ramp for someone who wants a smart home but has no idea where to start. We bundled the existing pieces into one hard bundle and built the whole thing around that one job.

And it worked with almost nothing behind it. No creative budget. The imagery was produced entirely with generative AI and CGI instead of a photo shoot. No paid media, so the reach and conversion were all organic. It sits at 4.6 stars, and customers genuinely love it.

It's a proof point of what positioning alone can do. Same products, sharper idea: a single-minded promise plus a clear picture of who it's for turned a set of existing SKUs into something people actively choose.

Feature marketing & launch

Announcing Smart Home's Next AI Features

Sept 2025 · NYC Alexa+ Smart Home announce Echo × Ring Fire TV × Ring

In September 2025, I led the announce strategy and planning for a slate of new Alexa+ Smart Home features at Amazon's fall Devices & Services event in New York. I owned the Alexa+ Smart Home portion specifically, not the full event.

The features are some of the most forward-leaning consumer AI we have brought to market: new camera intelligence that connects Ring with Echo, and Ring with Fire TV, so AI-powered camera features surface right on the screens already in people's homes. These are capabilities customers are seeing for the first time anywhere, and the announce had to make that land without drowning in technical detail.

My job was to shape how the story came across: what we led with, how we sequenced the features, and how we made never-before-seen AI feel immediately useful instead of abstract. I also helped refresh the pages where customers go to discover these features.
